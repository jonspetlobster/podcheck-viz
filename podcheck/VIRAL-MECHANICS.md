## The Podcaster's Dilemma (Game Theory)

This is where it gets interesting. Once PodCheck has traction:

**If you're accurate (A/B grade):**
- You promote your score loudly
- You reference it on air
- You link it everywhere
- You become a PodCheck evangelist (free marketing)

**If you're inaccurate (D/F grade):**
- You can't ignore it â€” your competitors will reference it
- Attacking PodCheck makes you look defensive ("why are you mad about fact-checking?")
- Your best move is to **improve** â€” check your facts, issue corrections
- Or engage with the dispute system (which drives more traffic to PodCheck)

**There is no winning move against PodCheck except being more accurate.** That's the beauty.

### The Streisand Effect Trap
If a podcaster with a bad score attacks PodCheck publicly:
- It drives traffic to PodCheck
- People look up the score
- They see the evidence
- Net effect: more users, more awareness

The only people who would want to shut PodCheck down are people with bad scores. And their opposition validates the product.
